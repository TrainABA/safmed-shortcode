FK-11 Environment, Stimulus, Stimulus Class

SAFMED = Say All Fast, Minute Each Day
[start]

[q] Define environment
[a] “The conglomerate of real circumstances in which the organism or reference part of the organism exists; behavior cannot occur in the absence of environment” (Cooper, Heron, & Heward, 2007, p. 694).[q] Define stimulus
[a] “Any physical event, combination of events, or relation among events” (Catania, 2013, p. 466). [q] Give two definitions of stimulus class
[a] 1. Stimulus class - “A group of stimuli that share specified common elements along formal (e.g. size, color), temporal (e.g. antecedent or consequent), and/or functional (e.g., discriminative stimulus) dimensions” (Cooper, Heron, & Heward, 2007, p. 705).  2. “Any group of stimuli sharing a predetermined set of common elements in one or more of these dimensions” (Cooper, Heron, & Heward, 2007, p. 27).
