[q]Define mentalism. (Cooper, Heron, Heward, 2007, p. 699)

[a] “An approach to explaining behavior that assumes that a mental, or ‘inner,’ dimension exists that differs from a behavioral dimension and that phenomena in this dimension either directly cause or at least mediate some forms of behavior, if not all” (Cooper, Heron, Heward, 2007, p. 699).
